# Voice and Tense Patterns

## Voice Distribution
- Overall passive voice ratio: 21.5%
- Passive voice is more prevalent in Methods and Results sections
- Active voice is used for describing motivations and established facts

## Section-Specific Tense Patterns

### Introduction
- **Present tense** (56.8%): General facts, current state of knowledge, motivation
  - "Hydrogen has a wide flammability range"
  - "Spark-ignition engines are suitable for..."
  - "The thermal efficiency of a hydrogen engine can also be increased by..."
- **Past tense** (38.5%): Literature review, previous findings
  - "Verhelst et al. [7] indicated that..."
  - "Previous studies have shown that..."
  - "Lee et al. [12] reported that backfire could be suppressed by..."
- **Future** (4.7%): Study objectives
  - "This paper will focus on..."

### Methods / Experimental Setup
- **Past tense** (37.5%): Description of what was done
  - "The engine was equipped with..."
  - "The experiments were conducted at..."
  - "A single-cylinder SI engine was employed in this study"
- **Present tense** (56.6%): Description of apparatus and setups (as they exist)
  - "Table 2 lists the engine specifications"
  - "The test engine is a six-cylinder, heavy-duty diesel engine"

### Results and Discussion
- **Past tense** (48.9%): Reporting experimental findings
  - "The gross ITE improved as the compression ratio was increased"
  - "Backfire occurred as the excess air ratio decreased"
  - "A high BTE of 42.8% was achieved"
- **Present tense** (48.2%): Figure/table references and established relationships
  - "Fig. 5 shows the heat release rate curves"
  - "As shown in Fig. 10, the NOX emissions decrease with..."

### Conclusion
- **Past tense** (46.7%): Summarizing findings
  - "High compression ratios and intake boosting were employed"
  - "The gross ITE under lean combustion was 18.4% higher than..."
- **Present tense** (47.0%): Implications and general statements
  - "Stoichiometric operation using syngas is appropriate for..."

## Key Voice Patterns

### Passive Voice (prefer in Methods/Results)
- "was investigated" / "were investigated"
- "was achieved" / "were achieved"
- "was conducted" / "were conducted"
- "was employed" / "were employed"
- "was observed" / "were observed"
- "was selected" / "were selected"
- "can be reduced" / "could be suppressed"

### Active Voice (for describing phenomena)
- "The thermal efficiency improved..."
- "The NOX emissions decreased with..."
- "Backfire occurrence reduces the volumetric efficiency"
- "The strategy increased the degree of constant volume combustion"
