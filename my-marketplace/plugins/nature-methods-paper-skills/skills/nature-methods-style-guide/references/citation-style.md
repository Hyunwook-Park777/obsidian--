# Citation Style Guide

## Format: Numbered Brackets

### In-Text Citations
- Single: [1]
- Multiple separate: [1,2]
- Range: [4-6]
- Mixed: [1,3,5-7]
- With author: "Verhelst et al. [7] indicated that..."
- Without author: "...as reported in previous studies [8,9]"

### Author Mention Patterns
- First mention: "Sahoo et al. conducted an experimental study [10]"
- Subsequent: "The authors reported that..."
- Multiple authors: "Park et al. [23]" / "Lee et al. [12]"
- Single author: "Sahoo [10]" is rare; typically "Sahoo et al. [10]"

### Placement
- Before period: "...resulting in a power drop [10]."
- After verb: "Verhelst et al. [7] indicated that..."
- Multiple at end: "...in previous studies [8,9,15]."

### Reference List Format (Elsevier journals)
```
[1] Author AB, Author CD, Author EF. Title of the paper. Journal Name
    Year;Volume(Issue):Pages. https://doi.org/xxx.
```

Example:
```
[1] Fiore M, Magi V, Viggiano A. Internal combustion engines powered by
    syngas: A review. Appl Energy 2020;276:115415.
    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apenergy.2020.115415.
```

### Journal Abbreviations
- Energy Conversion and Management → Energy Convers Manage
- International Journal of Hydrogen Energy → Int J Hydrogen Energy
- Applied Energy → Appl Energy
- Applied Thermal Engineering → Appl Therm Eng

## Best Practices
1. Number references in order of appearance
2. Cite original source, not review when specific data is referenced
3. Use "et al." for 3+ authors in text
4. Include DOI when available
5. Typical paper has 25-40 references
