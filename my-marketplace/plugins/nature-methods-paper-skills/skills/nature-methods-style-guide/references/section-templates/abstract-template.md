# Abstract Template

## Structure: Background → Objective → Method → Key Results → Conclusion

### Sentence Flow Pattern

1. **Background** (1-2 sentences): Present tense, establish context
   - "[Topic] is considered [significance/challenge] because of [reason]."
   - "[Current approach] has [limitation/advantage]."

2. **Objective** (1 sentence): Past tense, state study purpose
   - "The objective of this study was to [verb] the [topic] in [context]."
   - "In this study, [topic] was investigated to [purpose]."
   - "This study integrated [method A] and [method B] to establish [strategy]."

3. **Method** (1-2 sentences): Past tense passive, describe approach
   - "Using [technique], the [parameter] was determined."
   - "[Approach] was employed/implemented to [purpose]."

4. **Key Results** (3-5 sentences): Past tense, quantitative findings
   - "A high [metric] of [value] was achieved in the [condition]."
   - "The [parameter] improved as the [variable] was increased from [X] to [Y], owing to [reason]."
   - "However, the [parameter] decreased with an increase in [variable]."

5. **Conclusion** (1 sentence): Present/past tense, main takeaway
   - "[Condition A] was [percentage]% higher than [Condition B], mainly because of [reason]."
   - "[Strategy] were beneficial for achieving [target]."

### Example (from corpus)

> Synthesis gas (syngas) is considered an intermediate step between conventional carbon-based fuels and future hydrogen-based fuels. Spark-ignition (SI) engines are suitable for converting the chemical energy of syngas for small-scale electrical power generation. However, syngas-fueled SI engines have lower power outputs and thermal efficiencies than SI engines fueled with conventional fuels. The objective of this study was to compare the stoichiometric and lean combustion modes in a single-cylinder SI engine to determine the optimal combustion mode for the development of a syngas engine generator with high power and high efficiency. A high gross indicated power was achieved in the stoichiometric combustion mode by increasing the intake pressure. The gross indicated thermal efficiency (ITE) improved as the compression ratio was increased from 10:1 to 17.1:1. The gross ITE in the lean combustion mode was 18.4% higher than that in the stoichiometric combustion mode, mainly because of a significant reduction in the heat transfer loss. However, the gross indicated power in the lean combustion mode was 25.6% lower than that in the stoichiometric combustion mode.

### Length
- Target: 150-250 words
- Single paragraph, no subsections
