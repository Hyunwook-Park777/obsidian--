# Introduction Template

## Structure: Broad Context → Narrow Focus → Literature Review → Gap → Objective

### Paragraph Flow (5-8 paragraphs, ~2000-3000 words)

#### Paragraph 1: Broad Context
- Present tense for established facts
- Set the scene: climate change, emission regulations, energy transition
- Example opening patterns:
  - "With growing concerns regarding [topic], interest in [subject] is increasing."
  - "Technological changes in [field] have been led by [driver]."
  - "Efforts to minimize [target] are actively underway in various sectors because of [reason]."

#### Paragraph 2-3: Technology Background
- Present tense for technical facts
- Describe the technology/approach being studied
- Define key terms with abbreviations at first use
- Example: "[Technology] is a [classification] that [function]. In [technology], [process description]."

#### Paragraph 3-5: Literature Review
- Past tense for previous findings
- Author et al. [N] + finding pattern
- Group by subtopic or chronology
- Example patterns:
  - "Author et al. conducted an experimental study on [topic] [ref]."
  - "Author et al. reported that [finding] [ref]."
  - "Previous studies have shown that [conclusion] [refs]."
  - "The possibility of [improvement] has also been reported [refs]."

#### Paragraph 6: Gap Identification
- Transition with "Although" or "Despite"
- Example: "Although [previous work] have certain advantages, they also have some drawbacks."
- "However, [limitation] remains a challenge."

#### Paragraph 7: Objective Statement
- Clear statement of this study's purpose
- Example patterns:
  - "The objective of this study was to [verb] the [topic] in [context] to determine [goal]."
  - "In this study, [approach] was investigated to [purpose]."
  - "This study integrated [method A] and [method B] to establish [strategies] that [goal]."

### Key Writing Rules
1. Define ALL abbreviations at first use
2. Use numbered citations [1], [2,3]
3. Each paragraph: one main idea
4. Transition between paragraphs: However, Therefore, Although, In contrast
5. End with clear objective statement linking to methodology
