# Conclusion Template

## Structure: Restate Objective → Numbered Findings → Summary Statement → Future Work

### Opening Paragraph
- Restate the study objective and approach
- 2-3 sentences, past tense
- "[Engine/approach type] was investigated in this study."
- "The objective of this study was to [verb]..."
- "[Strategy] was established to [goal]."

### Numbered Conclusions
- Format: numbered list 1), 2), 3)... or with sub-numbering
- Each point: one key finding with quantitative support
- Past tense for findings
- Example pattern:
  ```
  1) [Finding statement]. The [metric] was [value], which was [X]%
     higher/lower than that of [comparison].

  2) [Finding statement]. As the [parameter] was increased from [X] to [Y],
     the [response] changed owing to [mechanism].

  3) [Strategy] was effective in [achieving goal]. The [metric] improved
     by [X]% through [approach].
  ```

### Closing Statement (optional)
- Summary comparison or recommendation
- "In summary, [mode A] is appropriate for [application A], whereas [mode B] is more suitable for [application B]."
- "Through the strategies proposed in this study, [performance] of [values] were achieved."

### Future Work (if included)
- Brief statement of planned work
- "The future work is to [verb] the [topic] by [X]% through [approach]."

### Example Conclusion (from corpus)

> This study investigated strategies for hydrogen-air mixture formation to suppress backfire, pre-ignition, and knock in full-load operations of a hydrogen PFI engine. The main conclusions of this study are as follows.
>
> 1) As the hydrogen injection pressure increased from 0.8 to 1.2 MPa, the spray tip penetration during the development phase increased owing to the high spray momentum.
>
> 2) Backfire occurred at an excess air ratio of approximately 2.1 at the maximum torque. The backfire occurrence in a particular cylinder caused knock and pre-ignition in the subsequent firing-order cylinders.
>
> 3) When adopting early hydrogen SOIs, there were no significant changes in the HRR and net IMEP with varying hydrogen injection pressures at the maximum torque.
>
> Through the strategies for hydrogen-air mixture formation proposed in this study, high engine loads of 1.95 MPa and 1.64 MPa net IMEPs were achieved at the maximum torque and power, respectively.

### Length
- Target: 300-500 words
- Typically 3-6 numbered conclusions
