# Results and Discussion Template

## Structure: Parametric Study → Data Presentation → Analysis → Comparison

### Typical Subsection Patterns

#### 3.1 First Parameter Study
- Start with figure/table reference
- "Fig. [N] shows [quantity] as a function of [variable] at [condition]."
- "As shown in Fig. [N], the [parameter] increased/decreased with an increase in [variable]."
- Explain the physical mechanism: "This is because [mechanism]."
- Quantify: "The [parameter] improved by [X]% compared to [baseline]."

#### 3.2 Second Parameter Study
- Systematic variation of next parameter
- Compare with first study results
- "When the [parameter] was increased from [X] to [Y], the [response] changed by [Z]%."

#### 3.3 Comparison / Optimal Conditions
- Compare modes/conditions
- Energy balance analysis
- "The optimal [parameter] was determined to be [value]."
- "Based on a parametric study, the [condition] was selected as [value]."

### Key Writing Patterns

#### Figure References
- "Fig. [N] shows..." (most common opener)
- "As shown in Fig. [N],..."
- "Fig. [N] presents the [quantity] curves for..."
- "The [parameter] is plotted against [variable] in Fig. [N]."

#### Quantitative Reporting
- "A high [metric] of [value] was achieved at [condition]."
- "The [metric] was [value]%, which was [X]% higher/lower than that of [comparison]."
- "The [parameter] increased from [X] to [Y] as the [variable] was increased."

#### Causal Explanations
- "owing to the increase in [factor]" (formal)
- "because of the high [property]"
- "due to the reduction in [factor]"
- "This can be explained by the [mechanism]"
- "This is attributed to [cause]"

#### Comparison Phrases
- "compared to/with"
- "relative to"
- "was [X]% higher/lower than that of [comparison]"
- "the improvement was remarkable at higher loads"

#### Data Quality Notes
- "It is noted that..."
- "The [effect] was more pronounced at [condition]."
- "A similar trend was observed in [other condition]."

### Energy Balance Analysis Pattern
- Standard framework: gross ITE = 100% - heat transfer loss - exhaust loss - combustion loss
- Present as stacked bar chart (Fig.)
- "An energy balance analysis was conducted to investigate the sources of energy loss."
- "The low heat transfer loss was the main contributor to the improvement in gross ITE."
